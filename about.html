ABOUT WCS

WCS stands at the forefront of a critical mission: to conserve the world's most expansive wild areas. These 14 priority regions, encompassing breathtaking landscapes from the Amazon rainforest to the depths of the Congo Basin, represent a staggering 50% of the planet's incredible biodiversity. From majestic tigers roaming the Indian subcontinent to the iconic mountain gorillas clinging to the slopes of volcanoes, these regions teem with charismatic megafauna that hold immense ecological and cultural significance. However, unprecedented threats loom large – habitat loss, poaching, and the impacts of climate change threaten to sever the delicate balance of these ecosystems. Yet, WCS is uniquely positioned to address these challenges. Our dedicated staff, a powerful force combining expertise from field research, zoos, and aquariums, creates a synergy unlike any other. This allows us to gather crucial scientific data from the heart of these wild places, translate that knowledge into impactful conservation strategies, and educate the public about the importance of protecting these irreplaceable treasures. For over a century, this blend of science, hands-on conservation efforts, and engaging educational programs has driven WCS's achievements in safeguarding wildlife and wild places. Upholding the highest standards is not just a promise, it's a core value that guides our actions. Respect for the natural world, accountability and transparency in our operations, a relentless pursuit of innovation, a commitment to diversity and inclusion within our teams, a spirit of collaboration with local communities and governments, and unwavering integrity – these principles are the foundation upon which WCS builds a brighter future for our planet.


Our Work

By discovering innovative solutions to save nature, we can not only ensure the survival of countless species but also inspire a global movement to protect wildlife in the last wild places on Earth. This collaborative effort is crucial for safeguarding the delicate balance of our planet's ecosystems, which ultimately sustains all life, including our own.


Wildlife

Endangered species are animals and plants facing a very real threat of disappearing from Earth forever. Their decline disrupts the delicate balance of ecosystems, which are like complex webs where everything plays a role. From pollinating plants to controlling prey populations, endangered species contribute essential functions. By protecting them, we safeguard healthy environments that benefit us all, provide us with clean air and water, and ensure a future where these amazing creatures can continue to thrive alongside us.


Our Communities


Protecting Wildlife, Together: Deep within some of Earth's most pristine wilderness areas live Indigenous Peoples and local communities who have called these lands home for generations. These forests, grasslands, and reefs are not just landscapes; they are the foundation of their cultures, livelihoods, and well-being. At WCS, we recognize that these communities are the true stewards of these wild places, and their deep connection to the land is vital for successful conservation.

That's why we work hand-in-hand with Indigenous Peoples through our Global Rights + Communities Program. For example, in the Amazon rainforest, we partner with the Xavante people to co-manage a protected area, combining traditional knowledge with scientific expertise. This collaborative approach not only safeguards wildlife but empowers the Xavante to protect their ancestral lands and cultural practices. Through such partnerships, WCS strives for a future where wildlife thrives alongside the communities who share these precious ecosystems.


Our Solutions

The Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) tackles the issue of endangered species from multiple angles, employing a variety of solutions. Here are some key areas they focus on:

  • Protected Areas: WCS works to establish and manage protected areas, which are designated regions where human activities are restricted to conserve wildlife and their habitats. They collaborate with governments, indigenous communities, and landowners to create safe spaces for endangered species to thrive.
  • Anti-Poaching Measures: Poaching is a major threat to many endangered species. WCS implements anti-poaching patrols, deploys ranger teams, and utilizes technology like camera traps to deter poachers and protect wildlife populations.
  • Species-Specific Conservation Programs: WCS develops and implements targeted conservation programs for specific endangered species. These programs involve research on the species' biology and threats, habitat restoration initiatives, captive breeding and reintroduction programs, and community outreach to raise awareness and encourage local participation in conservation efforts.
  • Combating Wildlife Trafficking: The illegal wildlife trade fuels the decline of many endangered species. WCS works to disrupt trafficking networks, collaborates with law enforcement agencies, and raises public awareness about the devastating consequences of wildlife trafficking.
  • Sustainable Practices and Community Engagement: WCS recognizes the importance of local communities in conservation success. They work with communities to develop sustainable practices that minimize their impact on wildlife and empower them to become stewards of their natural resources. This can involve promoting alternative livelihoods, fostering eco-tourism opportunities, and educating communities about the value of endangered species.
  • Scientific Research: Strong scientific understanding is crucial for effective conservation strategies. WCS conducts research on endangered species and their ecosystems to gather data, identify threats, and develop evidence-based conservation plans.

By implementing these solutions and working on multiple fronts, the Wildlife Conservation Society strives to create a future where endangered species can recover and populations can flourish.

Get Involved

  • Donate: Monetary contributions are crucial for WCS to continue its vital conservation work around the world. You can choose a one-time donation or set up a recurring monthly gift.
  • Become a Member: Membership provides various benefits like exclusive content, invitations to events, and the satisfaction of knowing you're directly supporting WCS's mission.
  • Volunteer: WCS offers volunteer opportunities at its headquarters, zoos, and field sites (depending on location and qualifications). This allows you to get hands-on experience and contribute directly to conservation efforts.
  • Raise Awareness: Spread the word! Share information about WCS and the importance of wildlife conservation on social media, educate your friends and family, or organize fundraising events in your community.
  • Take Action: Stay informed about conservation issues and advocate for policies that protect wildlife and wild places. You can contact your elected officials or participate in petitions and campaigns supported by WCS.
  • Symbolic Adoption: This program allows you to symbolically "adopt" a specific endangered species and contribute to its conservation efforts. It's a great way to learn about a particular animal and support its protection.
  • Shop for a Cause: The WCS online store offers apparel and other merchandise where a portion of the proceeds goes towards conservation initiatives. You can support WCS while getting something cool!
